Nigeria's security landscape remains challenging with persistent terrorism, kidnapping, and banditry directly impacting SCIDaR operations. Government arrests of bandit leaders in August disrupted networks, while insurgents continue to control territories, limiting humanitarian access. Route disruptions persist and increase operational risks for staff.

NORTH EAST — HIGH RISK

Route Disruption / NGO Risk
Impact: Damaturu–Maiduguri route disrupted; operational risks for humanitarian workers.

Precaution: Limit travel to essential trips to Borno & Yobe (daylight only). Obtain security advisories, travel protocols and route clearance from the security unit before travel.

NORTH WEST — HIGH RISK

Mass Abduction & Highway Attacks
Impact: 50+ abducted in Zamfara; territorial control in Malumfashi; decline in farming & commerce.

Precaution: No night travel on major highways; security clearance required; share live GPS with trusted contacts; request escorts where recommended.

NORTH CENTRAL — MEDIUM RISK

Village Attacks & Bridge Collapse
Impact: 6 killed in Plateau; ambushes in Kogi; bridge collapse in Mokwa displaced 3,000.

Precaution: Maintain high situational awareness; avoid Abuja–Lokoja and other at-risk highway stretches; secure travel clearance.

SOUTH SOUTH — MEDIUM RISK

Foreign Targeting & Cyber Attacks
Impact: Kidnapping of foreign workers in Rivers; drug trafficking and oil bunkering increase.

Precaution: Reduce non-essential coastal travel; require security screening and certification; enhance cybersecurity measures for operations.

SOUTH EAST — MEDIUM RISK

Sit-at-Home Orders & Aid Worker Risk
Impact: 125% surge in certain attack types; disruptions on Mondays and targeted attacks on aid workers.

Precaution: Avoid travel on "sit-at-home" days (Mondays), avoid known IPOB hotspots, verify vehicle/driver security and consider escorts where required.

SOUTH WEST — LOWER RISK

Cybercrime & Rural Violence
Impact: Herdsmen attacks reported in Ondo/Oyo; Lagos cybercrime spike; cult activity in Ogun.

Precaution: Increase cyber hygiene and training; avoid rural travel at night; ensure security clearance for rural deployments.

Operational Impact Summary

The main operational impacts include slowed implementation, restricted humanitarian access, increased risk of staff kidnapping or attack, and reduced local commerce and farming activity. Present actions should prioritize staff safety and verification of routes and partners before deployment.

Immediate Actions
  1. Apply "Know Before You Go": check security advisory + route alerts.
  2. Limit travel to essential only; daylight movement preferred.
  3. Share itineraries and request security clearances for high-risk areas.
